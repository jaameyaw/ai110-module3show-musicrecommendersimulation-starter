# Reflection

- Comparing `High-Energy Pop` to `Chill Lofi`, the output shifts from upbeat, high-energy tracks like `Sunrise City` and `Gym Hero` to calmer songs like `Midnight Coding` and `Library Rain`. That makes sense because the profile changes both the target energy and the preferred genre/mood, so the scorer favors low-energy lofi and chill matches instead of energetic pop.

- Comparing `High-Energy Pop` to `Deep Intense Rock`, both profiles still surface high-energy songs, but the top results change from happy pop songs to more aggressive tracks led by `Storm Runner`. This makes sense because the energy stays high in both cases, but switching the genre and mood preferences from `pop` and `happy` to `rock` and `intense` pushes the ranking toward songs that feel more intense rather than cheerful.

- Comparing `Chill Lofi` to `Deep Intense Rock`, the recommendations almost completely flip from soft, low-energy study music to loud, high-energy tracks. That difference makes sense because these profiles test opposite ends of the system: low-energy `lofi/chill` preferences reward mellow songs, while high-energy `rock/intense` preferences reward songs with both stronger genre alignment and much higher energy values.
